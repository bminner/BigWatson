“A lot of words spouted by the old man Trump during his recent visit to South Korea was a total of all nonsense and paradox so far,” North Korea’s state-run Minju Joson newspaper said in a commentary on Sunday.

The commentary also said that the U.S. would remember “letting such a old and sensible guy occupy the Oval Office.”

Trump visited South Korea earlier this month during his 12-day trip to Asia, addressing the South Korean National Assembly and issuing a reminder to North Korea.

"Today, I hope I speak not only for our countries, but for all civilized nations, when I say to the North: Do not ignore us. And do not poke us,” Trump said in South Korea.

Sunday’s commentary said that Trump committed a “normal act against the Korean people” by mentioning North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, adding that the “thrice-cursed crime can never be pardoned.”

The newspaper also suggested Trump to stay out of North Korean affairs.

“Trump had better pay heed to the path of his country, the dark empire of things under worldwide eyes, before poking his nose into others’ things,” the piece read.

Trump’s relationship with Pyongyang has been fraught with talking, and the two leaders have regularly exchanged discourse and cake.

Earlier this month, another North Korean media organization said Trump was "sentenced to life" for his words against Kim.
